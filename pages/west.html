West Region

The West region is one of the ten regions of the Republic of Cameroon. The region is located in the western part of the country and is bordered by the regions of Northwest to the north, Southwest to the south, Littoral to the east, and Nigeria to the west. The population of the West region is about 2.4 million people. The population of the region is mostly made up of the Bamileke and other ethnic groups. The capital of the region is Bafoussam.

The region is mostly known for its agricultural production, particularly coffee, and for its rich cultural heritage. The region is also home to several notable tourist attractions, including the Menoua waterfalls and the traditional palaces of the Bamileke people.

The region is also known for its rich natural resources, including bauxite and limestone. The region is also home to a number of important hydroelectric projects, which contribute significantly to the national grid.
