Littoral Region

The Littoral region is one of the ten regions of the Republic of Cameroon. The region is located in the south of the country and is bordered by the regions of Southwest to the west, West to the northwest, Centre to the north, East to the east and the Gulf of Guinea to the south. The population of the Littoral region is about 2.5 million people. The population of the region is mostly made up of the Bassa and other ethnic groups. The capital of the region is Douala.

The region is mostly known for its agricultural production, particularly cocoa, and for its rich cultural heritage. The region is also home to the famous Wouri River, which is one of the longest rivers in Cameroon. The region also has a number of important cultural and historical sites, including the Douala port.

The region is also known for its rich natural resources, including oil, natural gas, and iron. The region is also home to a number of important hydroelectric dams, including the Eda Dam, which is one of the largest dams in Cameroon. The region is also home to a number of important industrial sites, including the SONELGaz thermal power plant in Douala.
