Northwest Region

The Northwest region is one of the ten regions of the Republic of Cameroon. The region is located in the northwest of the country and is bordered by the regions of West to the south, Adamawa to the east, Nigeria to the west and the Central African Republic to the northeast. The population of the Northwest region is about 2.5 million people. The population of the region is mostly made up of the Bamenda people. The capital of the region is Bamenda.

The region is mostly known for its agricultural production, particularly coffee, and for its rich cultural heritage. The region is also home to the famous Ring Road, which is one of the most beautiful roads in Cameroon. The region also has a number of important cultural and historical sites, including the Bamenda people's palace.

The region is also known for its rich natural resources, including gold, uranium, and iron. The region is also home to a number of important hydroelectric dams, including the Bamenda dam, which is one of the largest dams in Cameroon. The region is also home to a number of important industrial sites, including the SONELGaz thermal power plant in Bamenda.
