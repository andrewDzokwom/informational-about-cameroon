Centre Region

The Centre region is one of the ten regions of the Republic of Cameroon. The region is located in the central part of the country and is bordered by the regions of Adamawa to the north, East to the east, South to the south, Littoral to the southwest and West to the northwest. The population of the Centre region is about 3.9 million people. The population of the region is mostly made up of the Ewondo and other ethnic groups. The capital of the region is Yaounde.

The region is mostly known for its agricultural production, particularly coffee, and for its rich cultural heritage. The region is also home to the famous Mfoundi River, which is one of the longest rivers in Cameroon. The region also has a number of important cultural and historical sites, including the National Museum in Yaounde.

The region is also known for its rich natural resources, including gold, uranium, and iron. The region is also home to a number of important hydroelectric dams, including the Yaounde Dam, which is one of the largest dams in Cameroon. The region is also home to a number of important industrial sites, including the SONELGaz thermal power plant in Yaounde.
