East Region

The East region is one of the ten regions of the Republic of Cameroon. The region is located in the eastern part of the country and is bordered by the Central African Republic to the east, and the regions of Adamawa and Centre to the west. The population of the East region is about 800,000 people. The population of the region is mostly made up of the Baka and other ethnic groups. The capital of the region is Bertoua.

The region is mostly known for its forest resources and timber production, as well as its rich cultural heritage. The region is also home to the Dja Faunal Reserve, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, which is one of the largest and most ecologically diverse protected areas in the country.

The region is also known for its rich natural resources, including gold and diamonds. The region is also home to a number of important logging concessions and timber processing facilities.
