Southwest Region

The Southwest region is one of the ten regions of the Republic of Cameroon. The region is located in the southwest of the country and is bordered by the regions of Northwest to the north, West to the east, Littoral to the southeast, and the Gulf of Guinea to the southwest. The population of the Southwest region is about 2.5 million people. The population of the region is mostly made up of the Bakweri and other ethnic groups. The capital of the region is Buea.

The region is mostly known for its agricultural production, particularly coffee, and for its rich cultural heritage. The region is also home to the famous Mount Cameroon, which is one of the highest mountains in West Africa. The region also has a number of important cultural and historical sites, including the Bimbia slave trade ruins.

The region is also known for its rich natural resources, including oil, natural gas, and iron. The region is also home to a number of important hydroelectric dams, including the Sanaga Dam, which is one of the largest dams in Cameroon.
