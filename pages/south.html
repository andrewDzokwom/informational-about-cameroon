South Region

The South region is one of the ten regions of the Republic of Cameroon. The region is located in the southern part of the country and is bordered by the regions of Centre to the north, East to the east, Gabon to the south, and Equatorial Guinea to the west. The population of the South region is about 2.2 million people. The population of the region is mostly made up of the Bassa and other ethnic groups. The capital of the region is Ebolowa.

The region is mostly known for its agricultural production, particularly cocoa, and for its rich cultural heritage. The region is also home to the famous Campo Ma'an National Park, which is one of the most beautiful national parks in Cameroon. The region also has a number of important cultural and historical sites, including the palace of the Sultan of the Bassa people in Ebolowa.

The region is also known for its rich natural resources, including oil, natural gas, and iron. The region is also home to a number of important hydroelectric dams, including the Memve'ele Dam, which is one of the largest dams in Cameroon.
