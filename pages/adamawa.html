Adamawa Region

The Adamawa region is one of the ten regions of the Republic of Cameroon. The region is located in the north of the country and is bordered by the regions of North to the south, Far North to the north, Nigeria to the west, Chad to the northeast and the Central African Republic to the east. The population of the Adamawa region is about 1.2 million people. The population of the region is mostly made up of the Fulani and other ethnic groups. The capital of the region is Ngaoundere.

The region is mostly known for its agricultural production, particularly cotton, and for its rich cultural heritage. The region is also home to the famous Waza National Park, which is one of the most beautiful national parks in Cameroon. The region also has a number of important cultural and historical sites, including the Lamido Palace in Ngaoundere.

The region is also known for its rich natural resources, including gold, uranium, and iron. The region is also home to a number of important hydroelectric dams, including the Lagdo Dam, which is one of the largest dams in Cameroon. The region is also home to a number of important industrial sites, including the SONELGaz thermal power plant in Ngaoundere.
